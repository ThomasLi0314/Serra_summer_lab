LCStool — A Complete Reference

Computing Lagrangian Coherent Structures in 2D flows with the ETH Zürich MATLAB toolbox (v1.0).

What is LCStool?

LCStool is a MATLAB toolbox developed by George Haller's group at ETH Zürich (Onu, Huhn & Haller, J. Comput. Sci. 7 (2015) 26–36). It extracts Lagrangian Coherent Structures — the most influential material surfaces organising transport and mixing in a time-dependent 2D flow.

Given any velocity field $\mathbf{v}(t, \mathbf{x})$, a spatial domain $D$, and a time interval $[t_0, t_F]$, the tool computes three families of LCSs:

FamilyPhysical meaningMathematical object
Repelling (hyperbolic) Most repelling material lines — the "spines" of stretching. Act as transport barriers. Shrink lines: integral curves of the minor Cauchy-Green eigenvector $\xi_1$, seeded at local maxima of $\lambda_2$.
Attracting (hyperbolic) Most attracting material lines — collect passive tracers in finite time. Stretch lines: integral curves of the major eigenvector $\xi_2$, seeded at local minima of $\lambda_1$.
Elliptic Closed material boundaries of coherent vortices. Outermost closed orbits of the generalised $\eta_\lambda^\pm$ field (null-geodesics of $E_\lambda = \tfrac{1}{2}(C - \lambda^2 I)$), detected by Poincaré sections.

Quick example — the three-line FTLE

If all you want is an FTLE map, the pipeline boils down to:

% 1. Eigen-decompose Cauchy-Green
[cgV, cgD] = eig_cgStrain(lDerivative, domain, resolution, timespan, 'incompressible', true);

% 2. Compute FTLE from the largest eigenvalue
ftleField = ftle(cgD(:,2), diff(timespan));

% 3. Plot
hAx = setup_figure(domain);
plot_ftle(hAx, domain, resolution, ftleField);

Ridges in the FTLE map pre-visualise the hyperbolic LCSs; vortex-like closed contours hint at where to place Poincaré sections for the elliptic analysis.

Theoretical background

The toolbox implements the variational LCS theory developed by Haller and collaborators:

  • G. Haller, Lagrangian Coherent Structures. Annual Review of Fluid Mechanics, 47 (2015) 137–162.
  • G. Haller & F. Beron-Vera, Coherent Lagrangian vortices: the black holes of turbulence. Journal of Fluid Mechanics, 731 (2013) R4.
  • K. Onu, F. Huhn, G. Haller, LCS Tool: a computational platform for Lagrangian coherent structures. J. Computational Science, 7 (2015) 26–36.
